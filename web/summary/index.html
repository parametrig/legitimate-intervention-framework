Executive Summary

14 February 2026 • Elem Oghenekaro • Dr. Nimrod Talmon

The Legitimate Intervention Framework (LIF) addresses the critical "time vs. legitimacy" trade-off in blockchain security. This summary provides a concise overview of the empirical findings and strategic recommendations derived from our comprehensive analysis of over 300 major DeFi incidents.

Read the full research narrative →

I. The Threat Landscape

Since 2020, capital loss in decentralized finance has exceeded $80B. Our analysis identifies "Logic Bugs" and "Access Control Failures" as the primary vectors, representing over 70% of frequency but often manageable if addressed within hours rather than days.

II. The Intervention Gap

Most protocols lack the governance speed to counter fast-moving exploits. LIF defines a tiers of "legitimate overrides" that allow for rapid containment without sacrificing long-term decentralization, moving from automated signer sets to social governance.

III. Measuring Efficiency

Our "Containment Success Rate" (CSR) metric reveals a wide disparity between protocols. High-efficiency protocols can prevent over 90% of potential losses through proactive monitoring and pre-authorized intervention sets.

IV. Strategic Framework

The transition from ad-hoc emergency patches to a standardized intervention framework is essential for the long-term viability of high-value protocol ecosystems. LIF provides the mathematical and operational blueprint for this transition.
